# 🏥 Healthcare Intelligence: Revenue, Efficiency & Risk Analytics

## 📌 Project Overview

Healthcare institutions generate enormous volumes of operational and financial data.
But data alone doesn’t improve systems — **insight does.**

This project transforms raw hospital admission data into a decision-support model that evaluates:

* Revenue concentration and scalability
* Operational efficiency through length of stay patterns
* Clinical cost drivers across conditions and medications
* Risk indicators reflected in abnormal test results
* Insurance payer mix dynamics
* Seasonality and demand fluctuations

Built using **Pandas for analytical modelling** and **Seaborn for executive-ready visualisations**, this project demonstrates how healthcare data can be structured into actionable intelligence.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement

Hospital leadership must continuously answer:

* Where is revenue truly coming from?
* Are some hospitals operating inefficiently?
* Which medical conditions drive disproportionate cost?
* Does payer mix create revenue instability?
* Are abnormal test rates signalling operational stress?
* What performance indicators should be tracked weekly?

This project answers those questions using data-driven evaluation.

## 🔍 What This Analysis Uncovered

### 📈 Revenue Patterns

* Revenue concentration among select hospitals and physicians
* Differences between high-volume and high-value institutions
* Admission type contribution to total billing
* Insurance providers contributing scale vs profitability

### ⚙ Operational Performance

* Length of Stay (LOS) distribution patterns
* Hospitals with extended stays relative to billing output
* Admission-type impact on operational load
* Time-based admission and billing trends

### 🧬 Clinical Cost Drivers

* Conditions that are high-volume vs high-billing
* Conditions that require longer inpatient stays
* Medication usage frequency and associated billing patterns

### 🧪 Risk & Quality Signals

* Distribution of normal vs abnormal test results
* Hospitals with elevated abnormal test rates
* Relationship between abnormal results, billing, and LOS
